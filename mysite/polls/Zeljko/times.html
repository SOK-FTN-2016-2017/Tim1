Nature notes

Goldfinches are gathering in flocks to feed on thistle seeds. William Cobbett in his Rural Rides (1830) described how in September 1826 he was going along a road in Wiltshire that had masses of thistles beside it. Flocks of goldfinches were feeding on them, and rose up and flew ahead of him, with others continually joining them as he went on, until he estimated that there was “a flock of 10,000 flying before me”. That sounds exceptional, but large flocks of goldfinches still gather in such places. They eat the seeds that are still on the thistle tops, and also those that have fallen to the ground beneath. When they are disturbed the air around fills with the flashing gold of their wing bars, and they may settle, with tinkling twitterings, in nearby trees. Some of the young birds among them still have grey heads, but many have already acquired the wonderful red, white and black heads of their parents.
